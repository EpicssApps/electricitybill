Google Sign-In and Google Drive Backup & Restore

Our Application provides an optional feature that allows you to sign in with your Google Account and back up your saved bill reference data to your personal Google Drive account. This section explains how this feature works and what data is involved.

What Data We Access Through Google Sign-In

When you choose to sign in with your Google Account, we access only the following:

  • Your Google Account email address — used solely to identify your account within the app and display your sign-in status.
  • Google Drive Application Data folder (appDataFolder) — a private, hidden folder on your Google Drive that is only accessible by this Application. We do NOT access, read, modify, or view any other files or data in your Google Drive.

What Data Is Stored in Google Drive

When you initiate a backup, the following data from your local database is uploaded to the Application Data folder on your Google Drive:

  • Bill reference numbers
  • Account numbers/IDs
  • Company names (e.g., MEPCO, LESCO, SNGPL, etc.)
  • Custom names/titles you have assigned to your saved bills
  • Associated bill type identifiers

This is the same data that you have manually saved within the Application. No additional personal data, files, photos, contacts, or any other content from your device is uploaded.

How Backup and Restore Works

  • Backup: Your locally saved bill reference data is converted to a JSON file and uploaded to the Application Data folder on your Google Drive. If a previous backup exists, it is overwritten with the latest data.
  • Restore: When you sign in on a new device and choose to restore, the JSON backup file is downloaded from your Google Drive Application Data folder and imported into the local database on your new device.

User Control

  • Sign-in is entirely optional — you can use the Application without signing in with Google.
  • You can sign out at any time from within the Application's settings (About screen), which disconnects the Application from your Google Account.
  • You can delete the backup data at any time by uninstalling the Application, which automatically removes the Application Data folder from your Google Drive, or by revoking access in your Google Account settings at https://myaccount.google.com/permissions.
  • We do not retain any copy of your Google Account email or your backup data on our servers. All backup data is stored exclusively in your own Google Drive account.
